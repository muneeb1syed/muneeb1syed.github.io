WPI Touch Tomorrow

The team headed to a nearby college called Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) for their “Touch Tomorrow” event. “Touch Tomorrow” is an annual, day long event that allows young kids who live in Worcester county to learn about STEM and Robotics for free. We were invited to the event, and brought a small section of the playing field along with a few plastic blocks to demonstrate the key features of CraterBot (our robot from last season). While some team members demonstrated CraterBot, the other members set up a larger field with mini soccer balls and blocks for our Soccer Bots. The Soccer Bots are two small robots the team built as an activity for the young kids at the event to play with. Using two Logitech game controllers, the kids could drive the Soccer Bots around a field and with the press of a button on the game controller, the robot would shoot the ball with the attached arm on the front of the robot.


In the previous year, the team created a ping-pong shooting bot that could be maneuvered and be used to shoot ping pong balls. We had setup a basket and created a challenge for kids to use the robot and fire the ping pong balls into it.
